# Macro Environment & Catalyst Assessment

WHEN to act. Regime classification, Fed transmission, catalyst hierarchy, seasonal patterns, and contrarian timing — everything about determining whether conditions favor entry, exit, or patience.

---

## Three-Tier Regime Classification

Regime determines position sizing and aggression. Classify the current environment into one of three states.

- **Risk-On**: CapEx accelerating + liquidity stable + no credit stress. Full position sizing, aggressive on high-conviction. Normal environment where bottom-up stock picking dominates.
- **Fear-Dislocation**: Market fear elevated but fundamentals intact. BEST buying environment (V1). "Best time to buy is on extreme fear when retail are selling." Supply chain thesis unchanged, price gifted.
- **Transition**: Mixed signals, regime shifting. Reduce sizing (x0.75), monitor closely. Do not force conviction during ambiguity.

### Regime Determination Inputs

Four pillars decide the regime:
1. **Hyperscaler CapEx direction** — THE leading indicator for the AI trade. Increasing = tailwind. Flat/declining = reassess everything downstream.
2. **Net liquidity trend** — directional indicator, not precision tool.
3. **Credit conditions** — stress in high-yield spreads, bank lending standards.
4. **VIX structure** — contango (normal) vs backwardation (stress). Elevated VIX alone is insufficient; combine with the other three.

If all four are positive: Risk-On. If fundamentals (pillars 1-2) are positive but sentiment (pillars 3-4) is stressed: Fear-Dislocation. If pillars conflict across fundamental and sentiment: Transition.

## Fed Transmission Chain

Rate changes transmit to equities through a 4-step causal chain. Each step is quantifiable, not sentiment-driven (V2).

1. **Rate change** — Fed funds rate moves
2. **Discount rate impact** — changes the rate used to discount forward earnings
3. **Forward earnings re-rating** — "Far out earnings of billions in revenue get marked UP" with rate cut projections
4. **Multiple expansion/contraction** — terminal effect on stock prices

**Rate cuts**: Triple cuts are historically rare (only 3 events in 25 years) and produce outsized moves in growth and small caps. Cuts benefit long-duration assets disproportionately — this is DCF math, not sentiment.

**Rate uncertainty as competitive filter**: A FEATURE for strong balance sheets. Destroys weaker competitors, concentrating market share among survivors (V1, V3).

## CapEx Cascade Model

The physical supply chain transmits demand in a specific order. Understanding this cascade is essential for timing entries across the chain (V3, V4).

**Transmission order**: Hyperscaler capex → Neocloud deals → Semiconductors → Memory → Substrates/optics → Raw materials

- **Hyperscaler CapEx is THE leading indicator**: "The biggest signal of whether the AI trade continues is hyperscaler spending." Everything downstream follows.
- **Validation chain**: TSM earnings = semiconductor demand proxy. TSM blowout confirms GPU demand, validates upstream thesis for substrates, memory, optics.
- **Overflow pattern**: When hyperscaler capacity is full, demand overflows to neoclouds. NVDA strategic backing validates this overflow. Asymmetric opportunities in smaller names.
- **Cross-company read-through**: META CapEx increase → more neocloud deals → validates downstream. Use one company's spending to front-run supply chain peers.

## Geopolitical Risk Framework

Geopolitical events are mostly noise, but some create structural opportunities (V1, V3).

### Tariff Shock = Buying Opportunity

"This volatility is often a 'valuation gift' since these are all empty threats." The TACO Trade (Tariff Anxiety Creates Opportunity) exploits the pattern where markets overreact to known information. Tariff tweets and announcements create short-term dislocation in names with zero actual tariff exposure.

### Export Controls Create Monopolies

When a supplier is banned via export controls, the Western alternative inherits instant pricing power. This is structural, not temporary — the removed competitor cannot re-enter. Identify the direct beneficiary and assess whether the monopoly premium is priced in.

### National Security Verticals

AI, Space, Energy, Critical Minerals, Quantum — these are government-backed theses. "Never bet against the White House." Policy tailwinds in national security verticals provide a durable floor under the thesis that pure market forces cannot replicate.

### Cross-Fire Casualty Hunting (V1)

During broad selloffs triggered by geopolitical events, unrelated quality names drop alongside exposed names. Fundamentals unchanged, price gifted. Pattern: Market sells everything on tariff news → quality name with zero tariff exposure drops 10% → entry point. Always verify the casualty has genuinely zero exposure to the triggering event.

## Liquidity Analysis

### Net Liquidity Formula

Net liquidity = Fed balance sheet - TGA - RRP. Use directionally, not as a precision tool (V2).

### Four Liquidity Drains

1. **Crypto precursor shock** — crypto often leads risk-off
2. **Fed policy uncertainty** — hawkish surprise or delayed cuts
3. **AI credit stress** — over-leveraged AI plays unwinding
4. **Carry trade unwind** — yen/dollar carry reversal

**Escalation ladder**: Single drain = noise, ignore. Two drains = tighten stops on marginal positions. Three or more drains = reduce leverage, maintain only highest-conviction positions.

**Post-liquidation entry**: After forced selling exhausts (margin calls, fund redemptions), quality names snap back first. Wait for selling to exhaust, then enter — this is the mechanism behind Fear-Dislocation opportunities.

## Seasonal Pattern Calendar

Seasonal patterns are tendencies, not certainties. Institutions front-run known patterns, which can negate or invert them (V1).

| Period | Pattern | Action |
|--------|---------|--------|
| **September** | Historical weakness, but consensus weakness can negate itself via front-running | Do not blindly position short. Assess whether the pattern is already priced in |
| **October** | Institutions begin front-running November buying | Identify tax-loss candidates for November entry |
| **November-December** | Tax-loss harvesting creates artificial selling in quality names | BUILD POSITIONS in quality names being artificially sold |
| **January** | Wash sale windows expire → mean reversion rally | Harvest the positions built in Nov-Dec |
| **Government shutdown** | "Historically stocks drop on news but increase near tail end" | IMMATERIAL — buy the panic |

The November-December → January cycle is the most actionable seasonal pattern. Tax-loss harvesting forces selling in quality names irrespective of fundamentals, creating the exact fear-dislocation conditions this methodology exploits.

## Catalyst Hierarchy

Not all catalysts are equal. Distinguish between events that change forward earnings and events that change only sentiment (V2).

### Real Catalysts (Material to Forward Earnings)

- **S&P 500 inclusion** — forced buying by index funds, structurally increases demand
- **Mega-contract or mega-partnership** — directly changes revenue trajectory
- **Government policy/executive order** — creates or removes market participants
- **Export control creating monopoly** — structural pricing power shift
- **Earnings beat with guidance raise** — forward estimates move up

### Fake Catalysts (Noise — Ignore or Use as Entry)

- **CFO resignation** — routine corporate event, usually priced in within hours
- **Conference presentation** — no new information, just marketing
- **Analyst initiation/upgrade** — lagging indicator, follows price action
- **Government shutdown** — immaterial to corporate fundamentals
- **Random tariff tweets** — TACO Trade; use the dip as entry

The test: "Does this change forward revenue?" If yes, it is real. If no, it is noise or an entry opportunity (V2).

## Six Macro-to-Micro Transmission Pathways

Every macro event reaches individual stocks through one of these pathways. Identifying the pathway determines whether the event is material (V4).

1. **Rate Cuts → CapEx Cascade** — downstream supply chain demand acceleration. Quantifiable via DCF.
2. **Export Controls → Monopoly Premium** — Western alternatives inherit pricing power. Structural.
3. **Tariff Noise → TACO Trade** — no real transmission to fundamentals. Entry opportunity only.
4. **Algo Earnings Misparse → Non-Fundamental Selling** — algorithms misread headline numbers, selling creates contrarian buy.
5. **Tax Harvesting → Quality Oversold** — artificial selling in Nov-Dec, mean reversion in January.
6. **Credit Stress → Weak Fail, Strong Survive** — market share shifts to companies with strong balance sheets.

For each macro event, identify WHICH pathway applies. Pathways 1, 2, and 6 change fundamentals. Pathways 3, 4, and 5 create entry opportunities without changing the thesis.

## Contrarian Timing

The methodology's edge is buying when others sell. These frameworks operationalize that principle (V1, V7).

- **Sentiment Inversion Rule**: News is data; sentiment direction is the inverse signal. When consensus is overwhelmingly bearish on a fundamentally strong name, accumulate.
- **Institutional Accumulation Detection** (V8): Smart money buying during panic is the strongest confirmation. Information asymmetry favors institutions. A data point supporting entry, not a directive.
- **Known Events Are Priced In**: "Institutions front-run rate cuts. Buy BEFORE the catalyst, not on the news." Position ahead of consensus catalysts, not on announcement day.
- **Three-Source Consensus Reversal**: When 3+ independent sources (analysts, media, social) are unanimously bearish on a fundamentally strong name → +1 conviction tier. Maximum pessimism = asymmetric entry.
- **Inverse Indicators**: Certain public figures serve as reliable contrarian signals at extremes. Use sparingly, only at genuine extremes.

## Proxy Analysis Framework

Use one company's data to validate or front-run another's thesis (V4).

Earnings from one company validate the demand environment for related supply chain companies:
- TSM blowout → confirms semiconductor demand → validates GPU supply chain names
- META CapEx increase → more infrastructure spending → validates neocloud and downstream
- Hyperscaler guidance raise → confirms AI investment trajectory → validates substrate, memory, optics

**Front-running via proxy**: When a proxy reports strong results, related supply chain names have not yet reported. The information is public but not reflected in the related name's price — a window for positioning.

After any major earnings report, ask: "Which other companies in the supply chain does this validate or invalidate?" Map the read-through before the market fully prices it across the chain.
